The Western Diocese of the Armenian Church of North America held its 99th Assembly at St. Paul Armenian Church in Fresno, marking a significant moment as the Diocese approaches its centennial in 2027. The event emphasized transitioning from strategic vision to concrete action across diocesan and parish communities.

## St. Mary Parish Representation

Our parish sent four official delegates:

- Very Rev. Fr. Yeremia Abgaryan (Parish Priest / Vicar General)
- Michael Hollis (Parish Council Chairman)
- Dr. Levon Keleshian (Elected Delegate)
- Puzant Zorayan (Elected Delegate)

Additionally, parishioner Mark Asdourian was elected to the Diocesan Council.

## Primate's Central Message

His Eminence Archbishop Hovnan Derderian framed the Assembly around the theme **"Toward the 100th Anniversary: From Vision to Direction,"** drawing from Proverbs 21:5. He highlighted three essential pillars guiding the Diocese:

- **Faith** — deepening spiritual relationships
- **Fellowship** — fostering supportive communities
- **Heritage** — preserving spiritual and cultural legacy

The Primate identified three focal areas for the next three years:

1. Evangelism
2. Community building
3. Innovation and Media Center development

### Four Parish Priorities

Every parish is called to:

- Strengthen spiritual formation through intentional programming
- Develop realistic, evidence-based strategic plans
- Cultivate stewardship and expand dues-paying membership
- Invest in leadership development across all levels

A significant initiative involves a comprehensive parish survey to assess strengths and growth opportunities.

## Stewardship Report

Executive Director Harout Markarian emphasized that stewardship encompasses management of "time, talents, and treasures." Key points included:

- Moving from stability to sustainability
- Achieving parish self-sufficiency
- Grounding decisions in data and honest assessment
- Aligning stewardship with mission outcomes

## Institutional Innovation

Dr. Hratch Tchilingirian presented a vision positioning the Church as "a way of life" rather than merely a destination. Four core principles guide this approach:

- Building a living relationship with God
- Integrating faith into daily life
- Embracing Armenian identity as heritage
- Diakonia (service as liturgy beyond the sanctuary)

Two flagship initiatives drive this work:

- **Innovating Our Parish** — revitalizing local communities
- **Innovation and Media Center** — leveraging digital technology

## Education & Leadership Mission

The Diocese operates robust educational initiatives:

- **Sunday Schools:** 18 parishes, 750+ students, unified six-year curriculum
- **Saturday Schools:** 16 parishes, 1,600+ students
- **ACYO:** Youth retreats, pilgrimages, service projects
- **Hye Camp:** Nearly 500 campers annually
- **New Day Schools** under construction in Glendale and Las Vegas

Leadership formation includes clergy training, altar server programs, lay ministry development, and extensive scholarship partnerships.

## Call to Action

Parishioners are encouraged to:

- Pray for diocesan guidance
- Complete the online parish survey
- Deepen engagement in worship and service
- Support stewardship initiatives
- Await direction from parish leadership
